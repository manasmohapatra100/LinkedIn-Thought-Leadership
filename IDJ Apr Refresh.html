The Most In-Demand Jobs of 2023

Authored byGreg Lewis

Sr. Content Marketing Manager @ LinkedIn | Data-driven storytelling, content strategy, and actionable insights

May 1, 2023

Co-authors: Co-authored byGreg Lewis and Co-authored byManas Mohapatra

Customer service is hard work. Too often, it doesn’t get the respect or recognition it deserves. From dealing with difficult customers to resolving complex problems, customer service requires a vast range of critical soft skills. But those skills may not be overlooked in the job market for much longer. 

That’s because it’s not just an incredibly demanding job — it’s also an increasingly in-demand job. 

Demand for customer service reps has doubled in just the past three months, making it currently the fourth-most in-demand job in the world, according to the latest LinkedIn data on paid job postings. 

Broadly speaking, frontline roles — those which must be done onsite in close contact with customers or coworkers — have remained in high demand, particularly in the retail sector. All this is occurring against a backdrop of historically high quit rates (at least among U.S. workers), according to the latest report from the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

What are the most in-demand jobs right now?

Whether you’re recruiting for these roles in particular or you’re searching for talent with strong soft skills, read on for the full rankings and reflections on the most in-demand jobs.

This story is updated on a quarterly basis to help talent professionals manage expectations, inform hiring decisions, and understand the changing landscape of the talent market.

Frontline jobs in healthcare and operations are seeing a spike in demand

Interestingly, the top 10 jobs with the fastest-growing demand are all new jobs, compared to last quarter’s list. As the world continues to recover from the ongoing pandemic, some key healthcare and operations roles are seeing demand double. 

Jobs with the Fastest-Growing Demand (QoQ)

Roles with the greatest increase in paid job posts, Q1 2023 (Jan. 1 – Mar. 31, 2023) vs. Q4 2022 (Oct. 1 – Dec. 31, 2022)

  1. Patient Service Representative 2.2x 
  2. Marketing Associate 2.1x 
  3. Beauty Advisor 2.1x 
  4. Home Health Licensed Practical Nurse 2x 
  5. Customer Service Representative 2x 
  6. Assembly Specialist 1.9x 
  7. Application Engineer 1.8x 
  8. Photographer 1.8x 
  9. Operations Officer 1.7x 
  10. Merchandise Associate 1.7x

In particular, patient service representatives have seen a 2.2x increase in job posts in Q1 2023 compared with Q4 2022, making it the role with the fastest-growing demand in the first quarter of the year.

Other frontline healthcare roles, such as home health licensed practical nurses, have also seen a significant increase in demand, with a 2x increase in job posts during the same time period. This could be attributed to the ongoing strain facing the healthcare industry — including “worsening mental health and well-being” and “healthcare worker shortages and burnout,” according to the World Economic Forum — as well as an increase in demand for home-based healthcare services, as many major economies deal with an aging population. 

Operations roles have also seen a boost in demand, with assembly specialists and operations officers seeing a 1.9x and 1.7x increase in job posts in Q1 2023, respectively. This could be due to a growing need for supply chain management and logistics roles as the global economy continues to rebound and restructure in the wake of the pandemic.

What about the customer service representative? While it’s only No. 5 on this ranking of jobs with the fastest-growing demand, it’s actually the biggest story here. 

That’s because it represents over 10x more job posts, in terms of sheer volume, than almost any other role on our first list. And that’s why you’ll also see it on our next list of the most in-demand jobs overall — in fourth place, immediately after the top three mainstays. 

Customer service reps become the fourth-most in-demand job overall on LinkedIn

The top three most in-demand jobs on LinkedIn have remained unchanged over the last three months, with registered nurse, salesperson, and software engineer continuing to hold the top spots. These roles have been in high demand for some time and show no signs of slowing down.

Most In-Demand Jobs (Q1 2023)

Roles with the greatest number of paid LinkedIn job posts, Q1 2023 (Jan. 1 – Mar. 31, 2023)

  1. Registered Nurse (no change in rank vs. previous quarter)
  2. Salesperson (no change)
  3. Software Engineer (no change)
  4. Customer Service Representative (+6)
  5. Retail Salesperson (no change)
  6. Cashier (+3)
  7. Project Manager (-1)
  8. Driver (-4)
  9. Store Associate (no change†)
  10. DevOps Engineer (-3)

This latest data shows several retail roles in high demand, which could be attributed to the rising consumer demand. Customer service representative and cashier jumped multiple ranks within the top 10 list from the previous quarter, while store associate makes its first appearance. 

Demand for drivers has cooled a bit in the past quarter, though it’s important to note that this comes on the heels of two consecutive quarters of sharp growth. A recent U.S. study found that over 80% of delivery drivers feel that they are underpaid, which could contribute to talent shortages and high attrition and, in turn, create more demand for the role. 

Final thoughts

Customer service roles may not be seen as the most glamorous historically, but the value of these workers seems to be rising quickly — at least judging by demand. With customer service ranked as the third-most in-demand skill in LinkedIn’s recent analysis, employers looking to fill job openings for these roles may continue to face fierce competition. 

Methodology

Based on global LinkedIn data on all premium job posts from October 2022 up to and including March 2023. The most in-demand jobs are those with the highest number of job posts in the most recent quarter (Jan. 1 – Mar. 31, 2023). The jobs with the fastest-growing demand are those with the greatest growth rate, with growth rate calculated by the number of job posts in the most recent quarter relative to the previous quarter (Oct. 1 – Dec. 31, 2022). Roles with fewer than 1,000 job posts in a quarter and roles for which the majority of job posts come from a single company were excluded from this analysis.

† The role of “store associate” shows no change in ranking because it was previously unreported, due to a majority of job posts coming from a single company in previous quarters, which is no longer the case.
